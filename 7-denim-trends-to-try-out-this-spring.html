7 Denim Trends to Try Out This Spring

Spring has nearly sprung, which means it's time to shed those bulky layers in favor of lighter, more relaxed pieces—this is where denim comes in. For decades, people have flocked to denim due to its durability and versatility. It has evolved from utilitarian workwear to a fashion-forward closet staple. From jeans to jackets to shirts (even hats), there isn’t an article of clothing that hasn't been rendered in cotton material.  

To be sure, denim has infiltrated our wardrobes and become synonymous with grab-and-go dressing. We wear so much denim that sometimes it can be difficult to imagine new ways to style it. But fear not; we've taken the latest spring denim trends — most of which involve classic pieces you likely own already or can easily invest in (denim is always a wise investment) — and created new and exciting outfits guaranteed to take your look from standard to stand out.

Scroll ahead to learn how you can upgrade your style by incorporating these latest styling trends. And remember: When it comes to denim, you can never have too much.

Light Layers

Levi’s dresses, on 34th jacket, Katy Perry Collections shoes and vintage hat.

Andy Jackson

When it comes to the unpredictable nature of spring weather, it's always a good idea to keep your clothing easy and breezy. That’s why wearing denim dresses is an excellent option. Try layering lighter and darker shades of denim for visual contrast and interest, then top things off with a bucket hat — also in denim, of course — for an extra touch of fun.

The Long and Short of It

Pairing a long denim skirt with a cropped jacket is a chic way to elongate your figure and effortlessly add a bit of sophistication. This winning combination is both modest and (thanks to the front slip) a bit flirty, which makes it a perfectly appropriate choice to go from day to eve with ease. What more could you ask for in an outfit?

Dress Code

Take a cue from school uniforms of yesteryear and bring your pleated skirt out for a spin. Pairing a pleated skirt (we personally love a mini moment) with a cute baseball cap and loafers gives a hint of academia to the look; topping things off with a denim duster adds dramatic flair. From sunny mornings to rainy afternoons, this outfit will always get an A+.

Block It Out

Why go for just one blue hue when you can wear them all? Choosing a head-to-toe color-blocked denim outfit is undoubtedly cool. Wearing a midi skirt over jeans is an unexpected—but winning—styling choice. Not only does this play-on color make matching accessories a breeze, but it’s also an easy way to stray from solids if you usually find bold prints intimidating.

A Romantic Touch

Denim for date nights doesn’t have to be boring. By wearing undone button-downs with a corset top, you instantly bring the blue hues to a dreamy, romantic realm. Plus, the corset's open neckline will showcase whatever bling you decide to wear. We recommend donning several delicate diamond tennis necklaces for some extra sparkle.

Patch It Up

Ditch your tired, plain denim jacket in favor of one with extra pizazz. The playful patches and bold brooches shown here lend a fun jolt of personality and visual depth to any outfit. When it comes to picking out your patches, go for ones that feel unique to your own style — and don’t be afraid to add as few or as many as your heart desires.

Pretty Tough

Piling on bulky denim pieces is a striking way to exude a tough exterior. Not only do the oversized layers provide protection from the elements, but they’re also a daring way to differentiate yourself in a sea of regular ol’ jean jackets. We love the idea of balancing these voluminous pieces with a pair of delicate denim ballet flats for a touch of ladylike charm.
