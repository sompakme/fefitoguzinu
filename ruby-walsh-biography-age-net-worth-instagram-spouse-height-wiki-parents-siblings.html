Career

From a young age, it was clear that Walsh possessed a remarkable talent. This was confirmed when he triumphantly secured the Irish amateur title not once but twice before reaching adulthood.

 With his innate sense of horsemanship and unwavering courage in the saddle, he swiftly ascended through the levels of his profession, showcasing his exceptional riding skills to all who observed him.

One of the most notable accomplishments in Walsh’s career was his triumph in the Aintree Grand National, which stands as the most challenging steeplechase event globally. He emerged victorious in this prestigious race twice, demonstrating immense bravery and expertise.

 In 2000, Walsh showcased his exceptional talents by guiding Papillon to cross the finish line first, while in 2005, he once again displayed his remarkable skills by piloting Hedgehunter to secure another remarkable victory. 

These outstanding achievements solidify Walsh’s status as a highly accomplished and respected jockey who excels in manoeuvring through the demanding obstacles of the formidable Aintree Grand National course.

Walsh’s career at the Cheltenham Festival established him as a dominant force in jump racing. With an impressive record of 59 victories, including multiple Champion Hurdles and Cheltenham Gold Cups, Walsh left a lasting legacy on the festival. 

His successful partnerships with renowned horses like Kauto Star and Hurricane Fly further solidified his position as one of the most accomplished jockeys in Cheltenham’s history.

Following a career-ending injury in 2019, Walsh decided to retire from riding. Since then, he has seamlessly transitioned into different roles within the racing industry, such as punditry, coaching, and bloodstock. Widely regarded with respect and admiration, Walsh continues to serve as an inspiration for aspiring jockeys.
