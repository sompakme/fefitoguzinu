Roberta Flack’s Early Life and Career

Roberta Flack was born in Black Mountain, North Carolina, and showed a keen interest in music from a young age. She started playing the piano at the age of nine and went on to study music in college. Flack’s professional career began in the 1960s, and she quickly gained recognition for her unique style and soulful voice.

Breakthrough and Success

Flack’s breakthrough came in 1972 with her album “First Take,” which featured the hit song “The First Time Ever I Saw Your Face.” The song topped the Billboard Hot 100 chart and won the Grammy Award for Record of the Year. This success marked the beginning of a long and successful career for Flack, who went on to release numerous hit songs and albums.

Roberta Flack’s Philanthropy

Roberta Flack School of Music

In 2006, Flack founded the Roberta Flack School of Music at the Hyde Leadership Charter School in the Bronx, New York. The school provides free music education to underprivileged students, reflecting Flack’s commitment to giving back to the community.
