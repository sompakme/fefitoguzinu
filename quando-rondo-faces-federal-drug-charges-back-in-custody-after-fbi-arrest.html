Quando Rondo Faces Federal Drug Charges, Back In Custody After FBI Arrest

In a turn of events, federal indictments have shed new light on the FBI's recent custody of Savannah rapper Quando Rondo. The new indictments reveal a web of criminal charges. They go beyond the state-level accusations of drug and gang activity that led to his initial arrest. Quando Rondo, born Tyquian Bowman, had been under the legal radar for months. This was due to state charges when he found himself in handcuffs again during a routine traffic stop. The unsealed federal indictments were disclosed on Monday. And they provided a comprehensive look into the extensive list of charges leveled against the 24-year-old rapper.

The skew of allegations includes conspiracy to possess with intent to distribute controlled substances. This includes drugs such as methamphetamine, fentanyl, cocaine, and marijuana. Moreover, Rondo is accused of engaging in the distribution of controlled substances. In addition, he's accused of possession with intent to distribute methamphetamine and fentanyl. Moreover, possession of a firearm in furtherance of a drug trafficking crime. Additionally, possession of a firearm by a prohibited person. And conspiracy to interfere with commerce by robbery. However, in total, Rondo faces a staggering 14 criminal counts.
