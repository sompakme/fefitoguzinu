Rodriguez Cline “Rod” Carew (born October 1, 1945) is a former Major League Baseball (MLB) first baseman, second baseman, and coach of Panamanian ancestry who played in the majors as a first baseman and second baseman. He played for the Minnesota Twins and the California Angels from 1967 to 1985, and he was selected to the All-Star game every season until his last one, except for one. Even though Carew was never known as a power hitter (just 92 of his 3,053 hits were home runs), he built a career out of being a reliable contact hitter throughout his career. 

He batted with his left hand and threw with his right. He was inducted into the National Baseball Hall of Fame as well as the Caribbean Baseball Hall of Fame throughout his professional baseball career. After his playing career ended, Carew went on to work as an MLB coach for many years.
