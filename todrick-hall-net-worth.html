Todrick Hall Net Worth

Todrick Hall is an American entertainer who rises to fame when he competed on the ninth season of a countrywide singing competition American Idol in 2010 and making it to the semi-finals. Hall capitalized on his success and perform on Broadway, he is also immensely popular on Youtube from songs, parodies, and skits he uploads on his Youtube channel which had over 3 million subscribers and over 800 million views.

Todrick Hall achieved success with no help other than his own hard work and perseverance. Realizing his potential, he established himself as an actor, songwriter, singer, and choreographer. From season 8 of the series RuPaul’s Drag Race, Hall became a resident choreographer and a judge on the show. Hall has a baritenor voice and performs in a mix of rap, R&B, pop, hip hop, and neo-soul — so far he has released four studio albums and an EP. It wasn’t until 2018, when he appeared on the Netflix series Queer Eye for the Straight Guy that the public came to know his true sexual orientation.

What is Todrick Hall’s Net Worth?

Todrick Hall’s primary sources of income are his music tours and Youtube channel. As of September 2022, Hall has 3.62 million subscribers and over 860 million views — it is estimated that Hall is making up to $16,000 per monthon YouTube. Todrick Hall’s current net worth is estimated at around US$4 million.

Who is Todrick Hall?

Todrick Hall was born in a middle-class household on April 4th, 1985, in Plainview, Texas. A single mother raised Hall. In his teenage year, his mother married and had a younger brother. Hall started learning ballet at the age of nine, and when at twenty, he was introduced to show business when he starred in the musical The Colour of Purple alongside R&B singer and actress Fantasia Barrino. Hall also appeared in Disney’s Beauty and the Beast and Radio City Christmas Spectacular musical stage show.

When he was 15, he openly admitted to his family that he was gay. At the age of 16, Hall took a job as a performer at a local Six Flags amusement park, where he combined dancing and singing in his performance. Hall also performed on cruises with Royal Caribbean, Holland America Line, and at Walt Disney Parks and Resorts. When he was in high school, Hall started composing his own songs as he enjoyed singing.

Career

Fantasia Barrino was the winner of the third season of American Idol; inspired by this, Hall auditioned for the ninth season of American Idol in Dallas, Texas, and received the green light from all of the four judges by singing a self-penned song. He went into Hollywood week and worked his way up to the semi-finals, unfortunately, he got eliminated in the top 16 round — his talent grabbed national attention nonetheless.

Hall amassed a considerable fan base, post-American Idol. He continued to use his popular YouTube channel to produce and post songs, videos, web series, and other content. Realizing the potential, Hall exploited the video-sharing platform to reach out to his ever-growing audience. Hall’s music videos comprised several of his original songs and collaborations with other contemporary artists. In November 2013, he starred in the pop music safety video for Virgin America, in which he wrote the song and lyrics. In 2014, he starred in his own MTV docuseries titled Todrick and was premiered in 2015 for 8 episodes. In 2016, Todrick self-released his second album, Straight Outta Oz. In October of that same year, he was chosen as Elvis Duran’s Artist of the Month for his music and was featured on NBC’s Today, where he performed live his single Wind It Up.

As Hall gained his celebrity status, from 2016 to 2017, Hall he began appearing as a judge on RuPaul’s Drag Race and frequently appeared on TV’s game show Gay for Play Game Show, Starring RuPaul alongside other celebrities. Also, between 2016 and 2017, Hall was in the Broadway musical Kinky Boots and appeared as Billy Flynn in Chicago. In 2018, in a follow-up to Straight Outta Oz, Hall released his new visual album titled Forbidden. To promote his new album, Hall embarked on a worldwide tour to Europe, Asia, Australia, New Zealand, and across the U.S.

In May 2019, Hall released his first EP, Haus Party, Pt. 1, with its first single, Glitter. In that same month, he announced he will be releasing a trilogy of his EPs within six months. In June 2019, Hall co-executive produced a music video for Taylor Swift’s song, You Need to Calm Down, and appeared in it.

On his Youtube channel, Hall has numerous musical tributes to singers who have influenced his work, including Lady Gaga, Rihanna, and Taylor Swift — for which he directed and produced most of his videos himself.

Personal Life

At age 15, Todrick Hall openly admitted that he was gay and expressed his desire to be a role model for LGBTQ and people of color.

In April 2016, Hall dated model Jesse Pattison and even adopted a puppy together, which they publicized on social media. The relationship lasted for a year, and they went their separate ways. In May 2021, he announced on his Instagram that he is in a relationship with model David Borum.

Controversies

In 2019, several of his former employees, including a choreographer and former assistant who worked on his Youtube videos, claimed they had never been paid. A link to a Tweet was shared by one of the accusers dated back to 2018, showing a lawsuit was filed against Hall by a former tour employee claiming sexual harassment, retaliation, and failure to compensate them for their work — Hall has denied these allegations.
