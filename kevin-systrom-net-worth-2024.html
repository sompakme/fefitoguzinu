Early Life and Education

Kevin Systrom was born in Holliston, Massachusetts, and developed an interest in computer programming at an early age. He attended Stanford University, where he graduated with a degree in Management Science and Engineering. His passion for technology and entrepreneurship was evident during his college years.

Beginning of Career

After graduating from Stanford, Systrom joined Google as a product marketer. However, his entrepreneurial spirit led him to leave Google and start his own venture, Burbn, which was the precursor to Instagram.

Creation of Instagram

In 2010, Systrom and Mike Krieger launched Instagram, a photo-sharing app that quickly gained popularity. The app’s unique filters and easy-to-use interface made it a hit among users, leading to rapid growth.

Instagram’s Success and Sale to Facebook

Instagram’s success caught the attention of Facebook, which acquired the app for $1 billion in 2012. This acquisition significantly boosted Systrom’s net worth and established him as a successful entrepreneur.

Role at Facebook

After the acquisition, Systrom continued to lead Instagram as its CEO. Under his leadership, Instagram introduced several new features and continued to grow its user base.

Departure from Instagram

In 2018, Systrom announced his departure from Instagram. While his exit was a surprise to many, it marked the beginning of a new chapter in his career.

Post-Instagram Ventures

Since leaving Instagram, Systrom has been involved in several ventures. He co-founded Rt.live, a website that tracks the spread of COVID-19 in the United States. His involvement in such ventures continues to contribute to his net worth.

Investments and Other Sources of Wealth

Apart from his entrepreneurial ventures, Systrom’s wealth also comes from his investments. He has invested in several tech startups, further increasing his net worth.

Real Estate Investments

Systrom has also invested in real estate. He owns several properties in the United States, which add to his wealth.

Philanthropy

Donations to Education
