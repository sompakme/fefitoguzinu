Golden Tate’s Early Life and Career

Golden Tate was born into a family of athletes, which played a significant role in shaping his career. College Career

Golden Tate attended the University of Notre Dame, where he played both football and baseball. His performance in football was particularly impressive, and he won the Biletnikoff Award in 2009 as the nation’s top college receiver. His college career paved the way for his entry into the NFL.

Golden Tate’s NFL Career

Golden Tate was drafted by the Seattle Seahawks in the second round of the 2010 NFL Draft. His NFL career started off on a high note, and he played a crucial role in the Seahawks’ victory in Super Bowl XLVIII. In 2014, he signed a five-year contract with the Detroit Lions, which significantly increased his net worth. He was traded to the Philadelphia Eagles in 2018 and then signed with the New York Giants in 2019.
