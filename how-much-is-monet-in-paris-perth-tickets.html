How Much Is Monet In Paris Perth Tickets

Monet in Perth? Is this some kind of Impressionist fever dream?

Nope! Buckle up, art lovers (and bargain hunters!), because Monet in Paris has landed in Perth, and it's bringing a whole lotta immersive goodness with it. But before you pack your berets and baguettes, there's one burning question: how much will this artistic adventure set you back?

Don't worry, be Monet-arily savvy!

Here's the lowdown on ticket prices:

  • The Standard Art Admirer: For a cool $55 AUD, you'll get the full Monet in Paris experience. Prepare to be dazzled by gigantic projections, lose yourself in the world of Impressionism, and maybe even smell a whiff of a croissant (not guaranteed, but hey, a man can dream).
  • The Concession Connoisseur: If you're a student, senior, or hold a concession card, you can snag a ticket for a slightly sweeter $49 AUD. Now that's a discount that would make Monet himself do a happy little dance.
  • The Tiny Art Troopers: Got a little art aficionado in tow (aged 2-15)? They can join the fun for a mere $44 AUD, but they gotta be accompanied by a grown-up guardian (no solo Monet missions for the wee ones).

So, is it worth the moolah?

Let's be honest, putting a price tag on pure artistic bliss is a tricky business. But with floor-to-ceiling projections, a chance to step into Monet's world, and maybe even a hint of a baguette aroma, we're thinking it's a pretty good deal.

Plus, here's a thought: how much would it cost to actually travel to Paris? Exactly. This is basically Impressionism on a budget, delivered straight to Perth.

How to snag tickets? Head over to the official Monet in Paris Perth website ([find monet in paris perth tickets ON monetinparis.com.au]) and get clicking.

How long is the exhibition on for? The exhibition has unfortunately ended as of June 25th, 2024. But keep an eye out for future art events in Perth! There's always something amazing going on.

How to dress for the exhibition? There's no dress code, so wear whatever makes you feel comfortable and ready to embrace your inner art critic.
